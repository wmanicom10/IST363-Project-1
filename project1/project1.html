A Brief History on Otto

Before Otto, Syracuse University's mascot was the Saltine Warrior, a Native American character that originated from a student newspaper hoax in 1928. For obvious reasons, the Saltine Warrior was retired in 1978 after Native American students petioned for its removal. A Roman Gladiator briefly took its place, but fans largely rejected the idea. The orange character first emerged in the early 1980s after SU cheerleader Eric Heath designed it. The name "Otto" wasn't chosen until the summer of 1990 at a cheerleading camp in Tennessee, beating the name "Opie." It wasn't until 1995 that Otto the Orange was recognized by Chancellor Buzz Shaw as the official mascot of Syracuse University.

Fun Facts:
  • His costume weights 10 pounds
  • Loves golf cart rides
  • Inducted into the Mascot Hall of Fame in 2023

About Otto

Otto the Orange is the beloved mascot of Syracuse University. Known for his energy and spirit, Otto has been cheering on the Orange since 1995!
